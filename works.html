About Me

Hi! My name is Amelia Austin, and I am currently a senior at
Carnegie Mellon University studying Decision Science and Human-
Computer Interaction.

Decision Science involves the application of scientific methods
and analytical techniques to make informed decisions. It encompasses
various disciplines, such as statistics, mathematics, and behavioral
science, to understand and optimize decision-making processes. HCI,
on the other hand, focuses on the design and interaction between humans
and computers, ensuring that technology is user-friendly, efficient,
and meets users' needs.

Combining knowledge in Decision Science and Human-Computer Interaction,
I aim to leverage data-driven insights and user-centric design principles
to innovate in healthcare technology. In the realm of healthcare, where
decisions can have a profound impact on patient outcomes, integrating these
disciplines becomes crucial. By understanding the intricacies of decision-making
in healthcare contexts and developing technology that aligns with the needs and
preferences of patients, I aspire to enhance the overall healthcare experience.
